Dear GSD Alumni,

Greetings from Berlin!

This semester, nine classmates and I are participating in a studio abroad program led by Frank Barkow MArch ’90 and Arno Brandlhuber. Our charge is to research new ways of dwelling and develop proposals for new housing for three sites throughout the city. Parallel to our studio research, we are taking seminar courses focused on the history of housing in Berlin, led by Fritz Neumeyer, and contemporary dwelling theory, taught by Niklas Maak. Through these seminars we have been able to reflect on the past and project into the future as we visit modern masterpieces of architecture here and in other parts of Europe, such as Berlin’s IBA ’57 development and Le Corbusier’s Villa Savoye.

Being in Berlin has been a wonderful shift from the trays of Gund Hall. By both living here and working with two leading practitioners in the region, we’ve been able to get real insight into the current culture and change that is occurring in this city and experience first-hand a city in the midst of transition. This experience will no doubt remain one of our best memories of our time at the GSD.

This week we are on our way to Dessau to connect with Professor Neil Leach and the GSD students participating in his seminar on Walter Gropius and the Bauhaus. We are excited to meet up with our classmates, and see, together, where it all began!

Christopher SooHoo
MArch ‘16
